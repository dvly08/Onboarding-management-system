Your Role & Responsibilities
----------------------------

**Title**: Support Contractor

**Core responsibilities**

- Contribute 40 hours per week during business hours, Monday through Friday.
- Provide high-quality email and phone support to Handshake customers.
- Typical day (once fully ramped):
  - ~90%: resolving tickets (questions, bugs, feedback, general inquiries).
    - Ticket volume: 20–30 tickets/day
    - Phone volume: 5–10 calls/day (once on phones)
  - ~5%: team meetings (1–3 per week)
  - ~5%: administrative tasks (email, announcements, training, etc.).

You’ll use demo environments, troubleshooting tools, our Help Center, Slack, and internal documentation to find accurate, helpful answers and deliver an excellent support experience.

Weeks 6–8: Phone Onboarding
---------------------------

**Objective**: Confidently handle support phone calls while maintaining excellent service quality.

You’ll be paired with a Phone Onboarding Buddy.

Week 1 (phones):

- [ ] First three shifts: Listen to live calls with your buddy.
- [ ] Last two shifts: Shadow your buddy and begin calling back voicemails with your buddy listening in.

Week 2 (phones):

- [ ] Reverse shadow sessions: you take calls, your buddy observes and coaches.
- [ ] Take scheduled Zoom calls with your buddy available to support.
- [ ] Be added to the phone schedule (not alone initially).

Best practices:

- [ ] Review call handling guidelines: understand root issues, provide clear solutions, set follow‑up expectations, and check in periodically if the caller is on hold.
- [ ] Review phone etiquette:
  - Update #support-urgent status at the start and end of your shift.
  - Mark calls/VMs you handle in #support-urgent.
  - If you end your day at 5pm, stop taking new calls about 10 minutes before shift end, then turn off phones at 4:50pm so you can finish on time.

Lessonly Courses:

- [ ] Phones Overview
- [ ] Let’s Talk Calls
- [ ] Zendesk Talk
- [ ] Phones 101: EDU

Onboarding Metrics & Expectations (Months 1–3)
----------------------------------------------

You are expected to be fully ramped by the end of Month 3.

Ticket Metrics:

- Average Handle Time (AHT)
  - Month 1: ~30 minutes/ticket
  - Month 2: ~20 minutes/ticket
  - Month 3: ~15 minutes/ticket

- Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)
  - Month 1: 85% or higher
  - Month 2: 90% or higher
  - Month 3: 95% or higher

- Ticket Volume
  - Month 1: 5–8 weighted tickets/day
  - Month 2: 8–12 weighted tickets/day
  - Month 3: 12–15 weighted tickets/day

- SLAs
  - Month 1: 90% SLAs met
  - Month 2–3: 100% SLAs met

- Quality Score (QA)
  - Month 1: 80% or higher
  - Month 2: 85% or higher
  - Month 3: 90% or higher

Training Completion:

- [ ] Attend all trainings and complete 100% of assigned Lessonly courses in Months 1–3.

Call Metrics (during on‑call shifts):

- CSAT: 100%
- Missed calls: 0
- Declined calls: 0

Contractor to FTE Conversion
----------------------------

As a Support Contractor, you’re typically contracted for 3–6 months and are eligible for our Contractor to FTE conversion program.

Conversion depends on:

- Availability of an open role/headcount.
- Meeting quantitative metrics (tickets, calls, training completion, QA, SLAs).
- Meeting qualitative expectations (communication, ownership, collaboration, values).
- Adhering to ticket and phone SOPs, guidelines, and the Handshake Code of Conduct.

Meeting these criteria does not guarantee conversion, but it keeps you in strong consideration when roles open up.

Handshake Code of Conduct (Summary)
-----------------------------------

All Support Contractors are expected to follow the Handshake Code of Conduct in all settings, including team events.

Highlights:

- No working under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or misused legal/prescription drugs.
- Zero tolerance for violent acts or threats of violence against employees, applicants, customers, or vendors.
- Maintain a psychologically safe, respectful environment for colleagues and users.
